## LECTURE 8

Object recognition. How to recognize novel objects from different vantage points? 
There are two competing theories, view-dependent and view-independent recognition.

Line drawings are impoverished relative to the real world but people of all cultures are able to quickly decode line drawings. Why are they interpreted more easily than a shaded mechanism? Line drawings require less time to read than 3D shaded versions. Thus line drawings may have an advantage when trying to communicate quickly. This makes them common in technical drawings. Photographs may not be the best choice. The more realistic view may not necessarily be the better option. Photographs are great for being true to life, but are indiscriminant in their use of detail. However, a good line drawing is hard and takes years of artistic training.

If you start a 3D model then you can drive the lines, since in a 3D scene there is depth information and can derive lines.

Pareidolia. Our vision is a meaning making mechanism. Be careful in our work not to trigger "the face mechanism". This capability is tied to a part of our brain, in the temporal lobe. Loss of this region is known as proposagnosie, or face blindness.

Visualization in Medicine
Stacking CT or MRI slices ontop of each other and pushing pixels into three dimensions creates voxels.

Molecular visualization is the most mature field. There has been a great deal of innovation and invention in order to solve the problems of visualizing something you cannot see.

Systems visualization involves the collective knowledge of a field. "Ome" refers to the collective knowledge. Ben Fry has been contributing to this field.

While there are good and different methods of visualizing things at the organ, tissue, cellular and molecular level, there is currently no integration of these different systems and databases. Ideally an interactive system linking databases on these scales would be combined.
